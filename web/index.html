Emissions-standard services (SAE J1979) that work on any vehicle through the gateway — no SGW bypass needed.

Guided check for the intermittent shifter-lock / forced-Park fault. The clue that it clears only by resetting the negative-terminal battery sensor points at power management (IBS), not the shifter itself. These steps use generic OBD-II — no SGW bypass needed.

  1. 1. Fault codes

    Read stored / pending powertrain codes and the check-engine status.

  2. 2. Power monitor

    Watch module voltage, RPM and run-time live. Voltage is sampled fast (catches the crank dip) and the lowest reading is tracked with its RPM. Keep it running through engine start and several minutes of idle/driving — the fault can hit at startup or minutes later. A sag when "Service Shifter" trips implicates the battery sensor / charging / connection path. Stop to download the captured log.

Comprehensive 11-bit enhanced sweep of the 500 kbps HS-CAN: probes every diagnostic address (0x600–0x7FF), then reads DTCs + module identification from everything that answers. Reading needs no security access — just the SGW bypass (for non-powertrain modules) and the key in RUN. Empirical: it reports whatever responds.

Covers the 500 kbps powertrain bus — where the TCM (and its U1267 shifter codes) lives. The 125 kbps interior bus (CAN-IHS: BCM, cluster, doors) is a separate pass.
